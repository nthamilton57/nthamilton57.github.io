My name is Noah Hamilton and I am currently a junior at IUPUI in the School of Science. I have always had a passion for learning about anything and everything. I have always had an interest in science, particularly biology and chemistry. I love looking for the intersections between all of these different fields and learning about their relations. Once I graduate from IUPUI, I hope to attend graduate school and get involved in research. Currently, some areas that I think would be particularly interesting to research are ecology and evolutionary biology and how this research can be used to better our treatment of the earth. I am currently working in the prep lab for K101 Biology led by Dr. Kathleen Marrs. Through this experience, I have come to learn that I enjoy and appreciate working in the lab setting. As a junior, I am working on my capstone research through Butler University where I am working with Dr. Julia Angstmann and the Center for Urban Ecology.

In my free time, one thing I love to do is read. My favorite genres are murder mystery, horror, and popular science books. Some of my favorites are Life of Pi, 11/22/63, When Breath Becomes Air, and Why We Sleep! I also enjoy running and playing pickleball with my family. I love watching sports, especially soccer and basketball. My favorite teams are Aston Villa, the Pacers, and the Colts. I also enjoy coding and am trying to teach myself python! Overall, I love learning about and trying as many things as I can!
